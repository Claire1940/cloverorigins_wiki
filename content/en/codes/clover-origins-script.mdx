## Early Game: From Trainee to Mage

Every journey begins in the starter village, where players must familiarize themselves with basic combat. Your first objective is to reach level 12 to 15, which serves as the gateway to the more complex systems of the game. During these initial levels, focus on the Bandit quests located near the spawn area.

While the combat may seem simple at first, it is important to invest your early currency wisely. Instead of hoarding gold, visit the local vendors to pick up basic equipment that provides a significant boost to your survivability and damage output.

| Item | Cost | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Katana** | Low Gold | Increases melee damage and provides a reliable backup to magic. |
| **Wizard Hat** | Low Gold | A base accessory that boosts mana pools for early casting. |
| **Combat Style** | Free/Base | The fundamental physical attacks used to finish off weakened enemies. |

> **💡 Tip:** Don't spend too much time on group mobs. Focus on the single-target quests to maximize your experience gains per minute during the first 10 levels.

## The Grimoire System and Rerolls

Upon reaching level 12 (though some players prefer waiting until level 15 for safety), you can obtain your Grimoire. This is the defining moment for any character, as it determines your primary magic type. The game provides a specific number of rerolls as you progress, but these are finite.

Every 15 to 17 levels, you are typically granted three additional rerolls. However, once you reach the level cap, these free opportunities cease. It is vital to use your **clover origins script** of progression to decide whether to stick with a common magic type or roll for something rarer like Anti-Magic or Lightning.

### Available Magic Types

*   **Regen:** High survivability with area-of-effect healing.
*   **Reinforcement:** Physical stat buffs and defensive maneuvers.
*   **Earth/Fire/Water:** Elemental damage types with various crowd control effects.
*   **Lightning:** High-speed attacks and stuns.
*   **Wind:** Mobility-focused combat with knockback abilities.
*   **Anti-Magic:** The rarest tier, focusing on nullifying opponent abilities.

## Mastery of Flight: The Broom Quest

At level 30, the world of Clover Origins opens up significantly as you unlock the ability to fly. This is not a passive unlock; you must complete a specific trial to earn your wings—or in this case, your broom.

1.  **Locate the Broom NPC:** Found in the outskirts of the initial village.
2.  **The Time Trial:** You must fly through approximately 16 magical hoops within a set time limit.
3.  **Activation:** Once completed, press **P** to mount your broom and navigate the humongous map.
4.  **Rerolling Brooms:** Much like magic, brooms have different skins and stats that can be rerolled if you have the necessary items.

## Mid-Game Boss Farming Strategy

The transition from level 50 to 115 is where many players stall. To maintain a fast pace, you should move away from standard "Bandit Beater" quests and focus on boss instances. The Bandit Boss is your primary target until level 50. After this, you should migrate to the more difficult boss encounters found across the map.

| Boss Name | Recommended Level | Key Drops | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bandit Boss** | 12 - 50 | Basic Gold, Exp | Easy to kite; use ranged magic and reset when health is low. |
| **Hog Boss** | 50 - 80 | Rare Materials | High health; requires consistent dodging of charge attacks. |
| **Orc King** | 115+ | **Goblin Boss Drop (Sword)** | Massive AOE attacks; best fought with a group or high Regen stats. |

> **Warning:** The Orc King has a specific drop, a powerful sword, that is essential for hybrid builds. Do not skip this boss if you plan on using a sword-magic combo.

## Advanced Magic Showcase: Regen Magic

If you happen to roll Regen magic, your playstyle will focus on attrition. While it may not have the raw burst damage of Fire or Lightning, its ability to sustain through long boss fights makes it a top-tier choice for solo players. Following a specific **clover origins script** for ability rotation is key to maximizing this kit.

### Regen Ability Breakdown

*   **Z Move (Ground Punch):** An explosive strike that creates a shockwave, dealing damage to all surrounding enemies.
*   **X Move (Auto-Healer):** A passive-active toggle that slowly restores health over time, allowing you to stay in the fray longer.
*   **C Move (Teleportation):** A quick dash that allows you to reposition behind enemies or escape a boss's ultimate attack.
*   **V Move (Combo Grab):** A dash forward that grabs the opponent, leading into a series of high-damage strikes.
*   **Ultimate (Regen Hospital):** Creates a massive forcefield. While standing inside, the user and allies receive massive health regeneration, effectively making the team unkillable for a short duration.

## Exploring the Humongous Map

The map in Clover Origins is vast, featuring iconic landmarks like the Giant Skull from the original series. As you progress toward the level 115+ content, you will need to utilize your broom to reach the Orc King's domain. The Orc King area is a high-density zone where the enemies are much more aggressive than the starter bandits.

When you reach this stage, your stats should be specialized. Avoid spreading points too thin. If you are a magic main, invest heavily in Mana and Magic Power. If you prefer the physical route with the Goblin Boss sword, prioritize Strength and Agility.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get more rerolls for my magic in Clover Origins?**

A: You earn three rerolls every 15 to 17 levels. It is important to note that once you reach the maximum level currently implemented in the game, you will no longer receive these free rerolls, so use them wisely before hitting the cap.

**Q: Where is the best place to farm after level 100?**

A: The Orc King boss area is the most efficient farming spot for high-level players. While the enemies are tougher, the experience gain and the potential for the Goblin Boss sword drop make it the superior choice over lower-level mobs.

**Q: Is there a specific clover origins script for flying faster?**

A: Flight speed is generally determined by your broom type. While you can't use a literal script to change base game speeds without risking a ban, you can reroll your broom at the Broom NPC to try and obtain a higher-tier version with better movement stats.

**Q: Can I change my magic after I've used all my rerolls?**

A: Currently, once your free rerolls are exhausted at max level, you may need to rely on in-game events or potential premium items to change your magic. Always try to settle on a high-tier magic like Anti-Magic or Lightning before you reach the endgame.
