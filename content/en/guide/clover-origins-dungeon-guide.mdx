## Dungeon Run Strategy: Clear Faster, Fail Less

Once you have keys, your goal is stable completion. Speed matters, but failed attempts erase the value of aggressive routing.

### Pre-run checklist

| Checklist Item | Minimum Standard | Ideal Standard |
|---|---|---|
| **Health sustain** | 1-2 sustain tools | Full sustain + emergency heal |
| **Damage profile** | Reliable single-target | Balanced AoE + single-target |
| **Mobility** | One gap-close or dash | Dash + escape option |
| **Inventory space** | Enough for boss drops | Cleared inventory before entry |
| **Team role clarity** | Basic focus target | Assigned aggro, burst, cleanup |

### Practical pacing model

1. **Pull discipline first**  
   Avoid stacking too many packs if your cooldowns are down. Controlled pulls often outperform chaotic speed pulls.

2. **Cooldown layering**  
   Use medium cooldowns on elite waves and reserve your strongest burst for boss phases where uptime is guaranteed.

3. **Positioning over panic dodging**  
   Keep line-of-sight and avoid getting pinned against walls. Most deaths come from poor angles, not low stats alone.

4. **Reset quickly after mistakes**  
   If your run pace breaks, re-stabilize instead of forcing speed. Clean runs build better long-term loot consistency.

### Common run mistakes (and fixes)

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | Better Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| **Using burst on trash immediately** | Leaves boss phase underpowered | Save major cooldowns for elite/boss checkpoints |
| **Ignoring spawn timing** | Misses key source windows | Plan dungeons around Yuno cycle |
| **Soloing undergeared** | High failure risk | Run duo/trio until survivability improves |
| **No drop tracking** | Poor progression decisions | Track key and fragment income per hour |

## Build and Team Recommendations for 2026 Dungeon Farming

Even without a fixed “meta lock,” certain role mixes feel smoother in dungeon content.
