## Common Mistakes That Slow Progress

Even with a good route, these errors can stall you:

1. **Skipping level thresholds too early**  
   Trying high-tier bosses underleveled leads to repeated wipes and lost rotation timing.

2. **Ignoring spawn cadence**  
   Random wandering creates downtime. Use timer-aware loops around 30/45/60-minute events.

3. **Over-focusing ultra-rare drops**  
   A 1% chase is fine as a side goal, not as your only progression plan.

4. **No fallback activity**  
   Always keep a backup farm (XP mobs, crystal raid, dungeon queue) when main targets are unavailable.

5. **Bad inventory/resource management**  
   Spending everything on rerolls can delay guaranteed power upgrades.

A complete **clover origins farming guide** works best when you mix RNG farming with deterministic gains. Keep one “luck target” and one “guaranteed target” active at all times.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best early route in this clover origins farming guide?**

A: Start with first-quest mob farming to level 50, get your grimoire at the tower, then farm to 100 before focusing on boss rotations. That order gives smoother damage and survivability scaling.

**Q: Should I farm Licht or Yuno first?**

A: If your build goal is anti-magic transformation, prioritize Licht windows. If you’re developing wind and want more dungeon access, Yuno often gives better overall progression because of orb and key potential.

**Q: Is dungeon farming worth it in 2026?**

A: Yes, especially for wind progression. Wind Spirit Fragments (10% drop) stack into Sylph crafting, and Sylph boosts movement plus combat efficiency, which compounds over long farm sessions.

**Q: How do I avoid burnout while grinding?**

A: Use timed rotations: boss windows, then dungeon or crystal farming, then XP refill. Alternating activities keeps progress stable and reduces the frustration of pure RNG sessions.
