## Clover Origins Storage Basics: What to Keep, What to Move, What to Drop

Before you optimize routes, set inventory rules. Your goal is to protect rare progression items while freeing slots for repeated farming.

| Item Type | Early Game Priority | Mid Game Priority | Storage Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Quest materials** | High | Medium | Keep until questline branch is done |
| **Boss orbs** | Very High | Very High | Lock/store immediately |
| **Dungeon keys** | Medium | High | Save for planned team runs |
| **Craft fragments** | High | Very High | Stack and store; don’t sell |
| **Common gear drops** | Low | Low | Sell/scrap fast |
| **Event currency** | Medium | High | Keep in dedicated tab/category |

A strong **clover origins storage** routine follows three buckets:

1. **Core progression items** (always keep)
2. **Session consumables/rotations** (carry limited amounts)
3. **Liquidation loot** (sell quickly)

> **Warning:** Don’t treat all “rare-looking” drops equally. Some low-frequency cosmetics are less impactful than materials tied to mobility, damage scaling, or transformations.

## Storage Priorities by Progression Stage

Your storage priorities should change as you level. Early game bag decisions differ from late-game optimization.

### Level 1-50: Clean Start, No Hoarding

At this stage, you’re primarily questing and farming basic enemies. Avoid collecting too many low-impact drops. Keep inventory mostly open for fast turn-ins and upgrades.

### Level 50-100: Grimoire and Build Direction

Once you unlock grimoire progression, item value shifts. Materials connected to your chosen path become premium storage targets.

### 100+ Boss & Raid Loop: Timed Drops Matter Most

After flight unlock and raid access, timing and repetition matter. Keep slots ready before boss windows so you never skip pickups.

| Stage | Main Activity | High-Value Stored Items | Inventory Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1-50** | Intro quests/farming | Quest essentials only | Over-hoarding junk |
| **50-100** | Grimoire progression | Build-specific mats | Keeping items for unused builds |
| **100+** | Boss loops/raids | Orbs, keys, fragments | Running out of slots during timer windows |
| **Late farm** | Repeated dungeon/event | Stacks of upgrade mats | Forgetting to liquidate filler drops |

This staged method keeps **clover origins storage** aligned with your actual objectives instead of random item collection.

## Practical Clover Origins Storage Layout for Daily Farming

You don’t need a complicated spreadsheet. A simple tab/slot layout is enough.

| Storage Segment | Suggested Capacity Share | What Goes Here |
|---|---|---|
| **Progression Vault** | **40%** | Orbs, fragments, rare boss drops |
| **Run Kit** | **20%** | Potions, travel items, current-use keys |
| **Event/Shop Currency** | **20%** | Black crystals and similar currencies |
| **Liquidation Queue** | **20%** | Items to sell/scrap after each loop |

### Daily loop (10-minute reset method)

1. **Pre-run check:** Empty liquidation queue.
2. **Boss window prep:** Keep 6-10 open slots before spawn time.
3. **Post-boss sorting:** Move rare drops immediately to progression vault.
4. **Dungeon session:** Stack fragments/keys together.
5. **End-of-session cleanup:** Sell all non-priority duplicates.

This structure makes **clover origins storage** predictable, especially when switching between Licht, Yuno, dungeon, and werewolf routes.

## Advanced Inventory Strategy for Wind and Anti-Magic Paths

Build specialization should affect what you keep. If you split storage equally across all paths, you’ll waste capacity.

### If you’re pushing Wind

- Prioritize Wind Orbs and Wind Spirit Fragments.
- Keep dungeon keys for coordinated burst runs.
- Reserve slots for Sylph crafting components first.

### If you’re pushing Anti-Magic

- Protect Licht Orb drops immediately.
- Keep rare raid drops separated from sellable gear.
- Avoid storing extra wind-only materials unless trading/use is planned.

| Build Focus | Must-Store Items | Medium Priority | Usually Safe to Sell |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Wind** | Wind Orb, Wind Spirit Fragments | Extra keys, mobility aids | Off-build duplicates |
| **Anti-Magic** | Licht Orb, rare Licht drops | General crafting mats | Common dungeon filler |
| **Hybrid/Undecided** | All transformation items | Small balanced stacks | Low-tier gear clutter |

For long-term efficiency, review your **clover origins storage** every few days and remove items that no longer support your current build goals.

## Common Storage Mistakes That Slow Progress

Most players lose progress speed from inventory habits, not combat skill.

### Mistake 1: Holding every “maybe useful” drop

This creates decision fatigue and blocks rare pickups. Set strict rules and follow them.

### Mistake 2: No timer-based slot preparation

If your bag is near full before boss spawn windows, you risk missing high-value drops.

### Mistake 3: Mixing progression and sell loot

Always separate “never sell” mats from liquidation items.

### Mistake 4: Overstacking keys without session plans

Keys are useful, but infinite stockpiling can crowd out rarer components.

> **Warning:** Don’t clean your bag while tired after long farming. Use a preset checklist so you don’t accidentally remove transformation or crafting pieces.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best way to start clover origins storage as a new player?**

A: Start with a 3-bucket method: progression items, active run supplies, and sell queue. Keep quest and build-critical materials only, then liquidate low-tier drops after each session.

**Q: Which items should I never auto-sell in Clover Origins?**

A: Avoid auto-selling transformation-related drops (like boss orbs), fragment-based crafting materials, and rare raid drops. These are often bottlenecks for power spikes.

**Q: How often should I reorganize my clover origins storage?**

A: A quick cleanup every farming cycle and a full review every few days works well. Session cleanups prevent overflow, while full reviews keep your stash aligned with your build path.

**Q: Is it worth keeping large stacks of dungeon keys?**

A: Keep enough for planned group or solo runs, but don’t let keys consume too much space. Balance key storage against rarer items like fragments and transformation materials.
