## Unlocking the Clover Origins Wind Grimoire

Acquiring the **clover origins wind grimoire** is a matter of persistence and luck. Grimoires are obtained through the Grimoire Gacha NPC located in the main town area. Unlike standard magic types, Wind is classified in the highest rarity tier, making it a rare sight among the general player base.

When you first start your journey, you will choose between the Good or Evil factions. While the progression paths for both sides are remarkably similar, your primary focus should be accumulating enough currency to roll for your magic. 

| Grimoire Type | Rarity | Primary Scaling Stat | Special Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wind** | Legendary | **Magic** | Includes a transformation Mode |
| **Anti-Magic** | Legendary | **Strength** | Awakening available via Lich Raid |
| **Lightning** | Epic | **Magic** | High speed and stun potential |
| **Darkness** | Rare | **Magic** | High damage, slow cast times |

> **Warning:** Unlike Anti-Magic, which scales with Strength, the Wind Grimoire scales purely with your Magic stat. Do not split your stat points into Strength if you intend to main Wind magic.

## Wind Grimoire Skillset and "Mode"

The **clover origins wind grimoire** is famous for its "Mode" feature. While many lower-tier grimoires only offer a basic set of moves, Wind allows the player to enter an empowered state.

1.  **Base Moveset:** These involve projectile-based wind blades and localized tornados that knock back enemies. They are excellent for clearing "trash mobs" during level-based quests.
2.  **Wind Mode:** Once unlocked, this transformation enhances your movement speed and adds additional scaling to your magic attacks. It changes the visual appearance of your character, often surrounding them with swirling green aura effects.
3.  **Mobility:** Wind users often have an easier time navigating the map even before unlocking brooms at level 100, thanks to the knockback and dash-like properties of their spells.

To maximize the damage of your **clover origins wind grimoire**, you must focus on the "Magic" stat every time you level up. With the current max level exceeding 3,000, a pure Magic build can one-shot most common enemies and contribute significantly to raid damage.

## Progression and Leveling Tips

To reach the point where your Wind magic is truly effective, you must follow the quest-based progression system. You can find the [official Clover Origins community](https://www.roblox.com/games/17094254413/Clover-Origins-BETA) to stay updated on the latest level cap increases and patch notes.

### Reaching Level 100 and Beyond
*   **Brooms:** Once you hit level 100, visit the Broom NPC. There is a short mini-quest involved, but once completed, you can press 'P' to spawn a broom. This is essential for reaching high-altitude areas like the Giant Skull.
*   **Trait Gacha:** Don't forget to visit the Trait NPC. Traits are unique modifiers that can further boost your Magic damage or reduce your cooldowns.
*   **Quest Tracking:** Always use the "Track Quest" feature in your UI. It will lead you directly to the NPC appropriate for your current level, ensuring you aren't wasting time on low-XP mobs.

## End-Game Raids and Bosses

Once you have mastered the **clover origins wind grimoire**, you will be expected to participate in raids. These are the only way to obtain high-tier accessories and items like the "Orb" used for awakenings.

### The Wolf Raid
Located in the **Lost Clover Valley**, this raid only triggers during a **Full Moon**. It spawns near the Mana Sense NPC. Wind users are excellent here because their area-of-effect spells can manage the multiple wolf spawns that can otherwise overwhelm a party.

### The Lich Boss Raid
This is currently the pinnacle of content in 2026. To access it:
1.  Go to the Grimoire Gacha area.
2.  Use your broom to fly up to the massive Giant Skull.
3.  Enter the portal to fight the Lich.

The Lich has a chance to drop an **Orb** and a unique **Accessory**. While the Orb is currently used for Anti-Magic awakening, rumors in the community suggest that a specific Wind Awakening questline may be added in a future 2026 update.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the clover origins wind grimoire better than Anti-Magic?**

A: It depends on your playstyle. Wind is superior for crowd control, mobility, and multi-target damage. Anti-Magic is generally better for boss-slaying and PvP due to its unique scaling and awakening.

**Q: How do I unlock the "Mode" for the Wind Grimoire?**

A: You must reach a specific level threshold (usually around level 500+) and potentially complete a mastery quest. Once unlocked, it significantly boosts all your magic-based stats.

**Q: Does the Wind Grimoire have an awakening like Anti-Magic?**

A: As of the current 2026 updates, there is no specific "Wind Orb" drop from the Lich raid for an awakening. However, the base "Mode" is powerful enough to compete with awakened lower-tier grimoires.

**Q: What stats should I prioritize for a Wind build?**

A: You should put the vast majority of your points into **Magic**. Some points into Health are recommended for survivability in raids, but since Wind relies on mana and magic scaling, neglecting the Magic stat will make the grimoire feel weak.
