While the massive Clover monster is the primary threat, the **trello clover origins** warns players of the secondary monsters: the parasites. These dog-sized, crab-like creatures fall from the main host's skin and act as a symbiotic defense mechanism.

In Clover Origins, encountering a pack of parasites is often more dangerous than the LSA itself. They prefer tight, enclosed spaces and can ambush players from ceilings or walls. Their blood contains high concentrations of the "Slusho" ingredient, which leads to a violent biological reaction in humans, including bleeding from the eyes and eventual internal combustion.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If a teammate is bitten by a parasite, they have approximately 20 minutes of gameplay left before their character "explodes." Use this time to complete objectives before they become a liability.

1.  **M1A2 Tank Shells:** Low effectiveness; primarily used for redirection.

## Exploring the Deep Sea Connection

A significant portion of the **trello clover origins** community discussion revolves around the "Deep Sea Drilling" theory. Before the events of the main game, the Tagruato Corporation engaged in deep-sea exploration to find "Katy Kanomitsu." This drilling likely disturbed the Clover monster while it was in a state of hibernation.

The falling object seen at the end of the original found-footage tapes—identified as the ChimpanzIII satellite—is often cited as the catalyst that finally woke the beast.
